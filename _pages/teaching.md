## Graduate Courses

### ECON 628: Topics in Applied Econometrics I
Advanced topics in applied econometrics for PhD students. Covers estimation methods for structural models, discrete choice models, and panel data techniques.

### ECON 555: International Trade
Graduate-level international trade theory and empirics. Topics include trade policy, firm heterogeneity, and global value chains.

### ECON 526: Mathematics for Economics
Mathematical foundations for graduate economics, including optimization, real analysis, and matrix algebra.

---

## Undergraduate Courses

### ECON 326: Methods of Empirical Research in Economics
Advanced empirical methods for upper-level undergraduates. Focus on causal inference, regression analysis, and applied econometrics.

### ECON 327: Introduction to Empirical Methods
Introduction to econometric methods including regression, hypothesis testing, and empirical applications.

### ECON 325: Introduction to Empirical Economics
Foundational course in empirical economics. Covers basic statistical concepts, data analysis, and interpretation of economic evidence.
